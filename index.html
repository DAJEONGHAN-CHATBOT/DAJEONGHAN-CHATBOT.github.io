한줄성경 is an AI chatbot designed to enhance the experience of searching for Bible verses. It provides emotionally resonant scripture-based answers to personal concerns and major life decisions, fostering faith-driven reflection while offering a comforting, interactive experience.

Abstract

"Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light for my path." — Psalm 119:105 ("주의 말씀은 내 발의 등이요 내 길의 빛이니이다." — 시편 119:105)

Throughout history, people have turned to faith in times of suffering, searching for meaning and hope. (오랜 역사 속에서 사람들은 고난 속에서 신앙을 의지하며, 삶의 의미와 희망을 찾아왔다.)

Christianity, in particular, has shown how the Bible can provide strength in difficult moments. (특히 기독교는 성경이 어려운 순간마다 우리에게 힘을 줄 수 있음을 증명해 왔다.)

Today, with AI advancing rapidly, chatbots and large language models (LLMs) can do more than just answer questions. (오늘날 AI 기술이 빠르게 발전하면서, 챗봇과 대형 언어 모델(LLM)은 단순한 질문 응답을 넘어설 수 있다.)

These tools can now offer wisdom, comfort, and guidance—helping people reflect on their lives and faith. (이제 AI는 지혜와 위로, 그리고 길잡이 역할을 하며 사람들이 자신의 삶과 신앙을 깊이 돌아보도록 돕는다.)

In this project, we introduce 성경챗봇, an AI chatbot designed to connect biblical wisdom with modern life. (이 프로젝트에서 우리는 성경의 지혜와 현대의 삶을 연결하는 AI 챗봇, 성경챗봇을 소개한다.)

More than just a tool, it is a companion—helping users find meaningful Bible verses that speak to their hearts. (이 챗봇은 단순한 도구가 아니라, 사용자가 자신의 마음에 와닿는 성경 말씀을 발견하도록 돕는 동반자다.)

By merging AI with theology, 성경챗봇 aims to bring faith closer to those seeking guidance in the digital age. (AI와 신학을 결합하여, 성경챗봇은 디지털 시대를 살아가는 이들에게 신앙을 더 가깝게 다가가도록 돕는다.)

Related Links

There's a lot of excellent work that was introduced around the same time as ours.

Progressive Encoding for Neural Optimization introduces an idea similar to our windowed position encoding for coarse-to-fine optimization.

D-NeRF and NR-NeRF both use deformation fields to model non-rigid scenes.

Some works model videos with a NeRF by directly modulating the density, such as Video-NeRF, NSFF, and DyNeRF

There are probably many more by the time you are reading this. Check out Frank Dellart's survey on recent NeRF papers, and Yen-Chen Lin's curated list of NeRF papers.
